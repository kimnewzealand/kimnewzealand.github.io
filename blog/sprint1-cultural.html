Overview of Values

I did some research into personal values and took some tests just out of interest. I took the DOPE 4 Birds Personality Types Test and it feathers me as the Owl. Logical, mathematically minded, methodical and sometimes seen as a perfectionist. I would add to that I am also see myself as efficient and can work fast to get things done but to a high standard, and compromise on perfectionism. For example when following recipes I will sometimes skip steps or ingredients to get it done.

    Explain a situation a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

    In my previous role at a startup, we needed more data for training one of our models. A suggestion was made in the wider team that we could combine client data just for the training. The collection and use of data is a complex and challenging area because we cannot always fall back on what is agreed or within the law. It is not black and white due to wider ethical implications. In that instance I was vocal due to an internal integrous voice to keep data separate in all scenarios to set a standard for ourselves as a team and company.

    Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

    My family is Dutch and some of my traits are very Dutch. I can be direct and to the point although this has probably been toned down after living in the UK for 13 years. I am also relatively organised person and like to be on time and schedule meetings in advance.

    Using the evidence gathered, describe your strengths and limitations.

    My strengths are curiosity and risk taker, I am always looking out for opportunities; balanced as I can bring calm and structure to chaotic situations; purposeful as I enjoy keeping busy, and can problem solve even in chaotic situations. My limitations are that I can get impatient with myself and get bored if I'm not busy. I should spend more time on self care.

    Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

    I have been able to change career to data science and teach myself to code by being curious and self motivated.

    Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they might intersect with learning obstacles.

    Although I enjoy problem solving in coding problems, I can get frustrated if I don't solve these quickly. I realise more and more to take constant breaks and to reach out to others to get different viewpoints and feedback.

    Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

    I have worked as a Test Manager in large IT programs. I worked on a website and app project at Sovereign which involved a variety of business stakeholders. My role involved understanding all project team members issues and perspectives to come up with solutions, and deliver under pressure. The biggest tension in the project was balancing the IT security standards feedback with producing a user friendly interface.
